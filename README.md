I am a Software Engineer. And I recently find myself interested in learning core programming. So, why CUDA progamming? Right?

We all know how fast the software industry has advanced with the introduction of AI / LLMs. As I make use of AI in my own job, I have come to accept it that AI **CAN** replace a software engineer. However, I stress on the word '**CAN**' because am not convinced yet that it '**WILL**'. Which means that there is always potential. After all, humans have always proved (or at least tried to prove) to conquer what they thought, at some point, would never be possible.

As an individual, I have always embraced change and am ready to see the world progressing in whichever direction we all take it together.

So, with my recently discovered interests in core programming and the direction in which world seems to be going, that is AI, I am convinced that it would be best to learn CUDA Programming. I believe that it would be the perfect middle ground as I would learn a lot of core development and at the same time expand my horizons in the domain of AI.

Now, we all are aware of [Leetcode](https://leetcode.com/) that allows use to practice our skills in DSA. Similar to this, [AlphaGPU](https://github.com/AlphaGPU) has come up with [LeetGPU](https://leetgpu.com/) to practice CUDA / GPU based programming.

I really enjoy grasping a subject by directly diving into problems - I believe that it is the most effective way to learn something. And I will use LeetGPU as a playground for me to learn this subject. As I do so, I will make notes on how I approached the problems and share it through this repository.
